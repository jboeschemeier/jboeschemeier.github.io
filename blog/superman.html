Superman on Glass? - Project Silica

  • November 26, 2019
  • By Jonas Boeschemeier

A few weeks ago, I stumbled upon the news that the “Superman” movie from 1978 was stored and readable on a few millimetres small piece of glass. Being a bit baffled what that actually supposed to mean, I dug a bit more into the topic and learnt about: Project Silica.

Project Silica is a research collaboration between Microsoft and the University of Southampton. It is essentially a new way of storing data; a way in which a piece of quartz class is altered with a laser that shoots in femtosecond intervals. What is a femtosecond you may ask? It is one quadrillionth of a second! The laser basically writes the data that should be stored on the glass and the pattern of the marks can then be used to read the data out. The original post that brought me into this topic talked about the original “Superman” movie being lasered on one such quartz glass. And indeed, now we can enjoy this old classic on a 7.5x7.7x2cm small glass coaster. The data stored on it accumulates to 72GB by the way, so quite a bit. The technology is not intended to be the smallest way of storing data, but to be a very secure and durable one. One that doesn’t require electricity to stay intact and that does not need complicated maintenance. For that reason, Project Silica will be most applicable in cloud-storage.
